What is eSports?

eSports, short for electronic sports, is a form of organized video game competition played between individuals or teams. Games like Call of Duty, Dota 2, League of Legends, and Free Fire are some of the most popular in the eSports scene. These events attract large audiences online and even in physical arenas, much like traditional sports. Professional players can earn money through sponsorships, streaming, and tournament prize pools. eSports is especially popular among young people and is growing into a billion-dollar global industry. It also offers career paths in coaching, commentary, content creation, and event management.

Game Description
Counter-Strike 2 (CS2) A tactical first-person shooter where two teams, terrorists and counter-terrorists, compete to complete objectives like planting or defusing bombs. CS2 is the latest upgrade to CS:GO, featuring improved graphics and gameplay.
Dota 2 A multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA) game where two teams of five players each battle to destroy the opponent’s Ancient while defending their own base. Known for deep strategy and hero variety.
League of Legends A popular MOBA game with fast-paced 5v5 matches, featuring unique champions with various abilities, focusing on team strategy, map control, and objectives to win.
Valorant A 5v5 tactical shooter combining precise gunplay with unique agent abilities, requiring teamwork and strategy to win rounds by planting/defusing bombs or eliminating opponents.
