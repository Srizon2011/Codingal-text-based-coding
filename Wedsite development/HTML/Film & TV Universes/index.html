Definition of each type of film and TV series

Action
Fast-paced movies with physical stunts, fights, chases, and explosions.
Comedy
Movies designed to make the audience laugh, often with humorous situations or dialogue.
Drama
Serious and emotional storytelling focusing on character development and personal struggles.
Romance
Stories centered on love and relationships between characters.
Horror
Intended to scare or disturb, often involving supernatural elements or psychological tension.
Fantasy
Includes magical, mythical, or supernatural worlds and creatures.
Science Fiction (Sci-Fi)
Focuses on futuristic technology, space exploration, time travel, and scientific innovation.
Mystery / Thriller
Builds suspense around crimes, secrets, or unexpected plot twists.
Crime
Centers on criminal activities, investigations, or the justice system.
Western
Set in the American frontier with cowboys, outlaws, and rugged landscapes.
Psychological
Explores mental and emotional states, often with intense or twisted narratives.
Historical / Period
Set in a specific historical era, often based on real events or cultural settings.
Musical
Features characters who sing and dance to express emotions or move the story forward.
Documentary
Non-fiction films that document reality, events, or people for informative purposes.
Animation / Family
Often animated films aimed at children or family audiences, with lighthearted stories and moral lessons.
